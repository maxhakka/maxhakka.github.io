A Case Management System.

An improved Case Management System with new features and functionality for a company owned by the City of Stockholm. The focus of this project was mergin two existing systems into one responsive website, with exactly the same functionality and usability both on desktop and mobile.

Background and Problem

The existing case management system that was used at the company was antiquited and lacked core features. Another problem was separate systems with different funcitonality for mobile and desktop, resulting in employees having to document things on paper while out in the field or to contact their collegues in the office who had access to the desktop version of the cased management system.

The aim of the project was thus to reduce the amound of manual administration for the company staff as well as modernising and simplifying the interface of the system. As the vast majority of the users accessed the system via their phones, a responsive and mobile first design was of particular importance in this project.

Research and Analysis

At the start of the project, a number of employees were interviewed in order to establish pain points as well as to map out the types of users for the system. A number of workshops were also conducted together with the company, where many types of users were represented.

A workshop was conducted together with the company, involving several different types of end users.

The workshops resulted in a number epics and of user stories as well the information architechure of the new site. A dasboard with an overview of the cases was the starting point, which would then link to a list of all the cases as well as facilites.

Ideation and Design

Based on the user stories nad the information architechure generated during the workshops, a number of low-fi wireframes were made. These wireframes were consequently tested on users using the think aloud method. Based on the test results, a number of adjustments were made and high fidelity designs created. The designs were based on grappic profile of the company.

The first low fidelity design of the dasboard made in Balsamiq.

A high fidelity version of the same design made in Sketch App.

The desktop version of the dasboard.

A mobile first approach was used when creating the designs, as the vast majority of the users are on mobile. This means focusing on creating the mobile designs first and then adapting them to desktops. This ensures that the amount of information is adapted to smaller screens first.

The high fidelity designs were then user tested as well and final adjustments were made based on the feedback from the user tests.
